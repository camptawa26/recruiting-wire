# The Recruiting Wire

*Week of May 11–15, 2026*

## 📌 The Week in One Paragraph

Cloudflare, Coinbase, and Upwork each announced workforce cuts within 72 hours of each other this week, all pointing to AI as the reason despite posting revenue growth. In the same period, investors committed over $1.6B to enterprise AI companies, and the Eightfold AI class action — the first lawsuit to allege Fair Credit Reporting Act violations by a hiring platform — advanced in federal court, naming over two dozen major employers. AI is now driving layoffs, investment, and legal exposure in the same five-day window, and they can no longer be tracked as separate conversations.

## 🤖 AI in Recruiting

- Eightfold AI is facing a proposed class action filed by former EEOC chair Jenny Yang and nonprofit Towards Justice, alleging the platform built covert candidate profiles from social media, location data, and third-party sources without applicant consent, then used them to generate candidate scores — in violation of the Fair Credit Reporting Act, the first such lawsuit to apply FCRA directly to a hiring platform. Employers named include Microsoft, PayPal, Morgan Stanley, Starbucks, and Chevron, all facing potential discovery subpoenas if the case advances. — [HR Brew](https://www.hr-brew.com/stories/2026/01/29/ai-powered-hiring-platform-eightfold-ai-faces-lawsuit-over-hiring-data-used-to-rate-candidates), [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/01/26/job-seekers-suing-ai-hiring-tool-eightfold-allegedly-compiling-secretive-reports/)

- LinkedIn’s Hiring Assistant drew fresh recruiter attention this week as VP of Product Hari Srinivasan appeared on Josh Bersin’s podcast and Hung Lee’s Brainfood Live to walk through where the product stands. Charter customers are reviewing 62% fewer profiles per role and seeing 66% higher InMail acceptance rates. February’s update added AI Applicant Targeting, AI Follow-Ups, and Microsoft Teams integration; the product has moved from a sourcing layer into end-to-end candidate workflow management. — [Josh Bersin Podcast](https://joshbersin.com/podcast/hari-srinivasan-explains-the-ai-powered-linkedin-hiring-assistant/), [LinkedIn Product Update](https://business.linkedin.com/talent-solutions/product-update/hire-release)

- Software engineer job listings jumped 30% in 2026 to more than 67,000 open roles across major tech companies — the highest tracked demand in three years — despite 52,000 tech layoffs in Q1. Companies are cutting general-function headcount while hiring for engineers who can work across AI, cloud, and security infrastructure simultaneously. — [Metaintro](https://www.metaintro.com/blog/software-engineer-job-listings-spike-2026-ai-demand)

- AI Engineer is LinkedIn’s fastest-growing US job title, with postings up 143% year-over-year. Roles listing at least two AI skills pay 43% more than comparable positions without them, and senior AI engineers now command $220K–$300K base at mid-tier companies. — [LinkedIn Jobs on the Rise 2026](https://www.metaintro.com/blog/software-engineer-job-listings-spike-2026-ai-demand), [Levels.fyi](https://www.levels.fyi/t/software-engineer/focus/ml-ai)
